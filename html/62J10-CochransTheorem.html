Cochran’s theorem


Let X be multivariate normally distributed as 𝑵𝒑⁢(𝟎,𝑰) such that

𝐗T⁢𝐗=∑i=1kQi,

where each

  1. 1.

    Qi is a quadratic form

  2. 2.

    Qi=𝐗T⁢𝐁i⁢𝐗, where 𝐁i is a p by p square matrix

  3. 3.

    𝐁i is positive semidefinite

  4. 4.

    rank⁡(𝐁i)=ri

Then any two of the following imply the third:

  1. 1.

    ∑i=1kri=p

  2. 2.

    each Qi has a chi square distribution (http://planetmath.org/ChiSquaredRandomVariable) with ri of freedom, χ2⁢(ri)

  3. 3.

    Qi’s are mutually independent

As an example, suppose X12∼χ2⁢(m1) and X22∼χ2⁢(m2). Furthermore, assume X12≥X22 and m1>m2, then

X12-X22∼χ2⁢(m1-m2).

This corollary is known as Fisher’s theorem.
